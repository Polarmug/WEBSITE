Gregorio "Gringo" Honasan II

Personal Information

Full Name: Gregorio Ballesteros Honasan II

Birthdate: March 14, 1948

Age on Election Day: 77

Birthplace: Baguio City, Benguet

Residence: Brgy. IVC, Marikina, Metro Manila

Sex: Male

Civil Status: Married

Spouse: Jane Umali Honasan

Profession/Occupation: Public Servant / Former Soldier

Political Party: Reform PH (2024–present)

Platforms

  • Advocates for land use reform, aiming to optimize the allocation and utilization of the country's land resources
  • Supports national security enhancements, focusing on strengthening the country's defense capabilities
  • Promotes the Freedom of Information (FOI) Act, seeking transparency and accountability in government operations
  • Endorses food security initiatives, aiming to ensure sufficient and accessible food supply for all Filipinos

Notable Positions

  • Senator of the Philippines (1995–2004; 2007–2019)
  • Secretary of the Department of Information and Communications Technology (2019–2021)
  • Chair of the Senate National Defense and Security Committee (2016–2018)
  • Chair of the Senate Peace, Unification and Reconciliation Committee (2016–2018)
  • Chair of the Senate Agrarian Reform Committee (2010–2016)

Senatorial Record

  • Authored/Co-Authored: Over 16 laws during his tenureship in the senate
  • Sponsored/Co-Sponsored: 12 laws covering a variety of subject including national security, environmental protection, and social welfare.

Notable Laws and Projects

  • As DICT Secretary, implemented policies to advance the country's information and communications technology infrastructure
  • As Senator, contributed to legislation aimed at national defense, agrarian reform, and peace processes
  • An Act Extending the Effectivity of the Rent Control Act of (Republic Act No. 8437):Provided temporary assistance to all renters by preventing further increase in rent for all housing businesses.
  • The Clean Water Act (Republic Act No. 9275): an act providing for a comprehensive water resource policy and water sector development, creating the water resources management commission and for other purposes

Controversies

  • Led several coup attempts against President Corazon Aquino's administration in the late 1980s, for which he was granted amnesty in 1992
  • Faced allegations of involvement in military mutinies and attempted coups during President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo's tenure, leading to temporary detention in 2006
