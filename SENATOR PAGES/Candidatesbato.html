Ronald “Bato” dela Rosa

Personal Information

Full Name: Ronald Marapon Dela Rosa

Nickname: Bato

Birthdate: January 21, 1962

Age on Election Day: 63

Birthplace: Santa Cruz, Davao del Sur

Residence: Brgy. Magtuod, Davao City, Davao del Sur

Civil Status: Married

Spouse: Grace Nancy Commandante Dela Rosa

Profession/Occupation: Government Official

Political Party: PDP–Laban

Political Platforms

  • Reimposition of the Death Penalty: Advocates for the revival of the death penalty for drug-related and heinous crimes, emphasizing its deterrent effect.
  • Support for Uniformed Personnel: Focuses on the welfare, modernization, and capacity-building of uniformed personnel, particularly those in law enforcement and emergency services.
  • Drug War Continuation: Continues to back the government's war on drugs, emphasizing rehabilitation and prevention.
  • Anti-Terrorism & National Peacebuilding: Strong proponent of anti-terrorism laws and measures aimed at promoting national security and peacebuilding.

Notable Positions

  • Senator: 2019 – Present
  • Director General, Bureau of Corrections: April 2018 – October 2018
  • Chief, Philippine National Police (PNP): July 2016 – April 2018
  • Chairperson: Senate Committee on Public Order and Dangerous Drugs
  • Chairperson: Special Committee on Marawi City Rehabilitation and Victims’ Compensation
  • Vice-Chairperson: Senate Committees on Finance, National Defense and Security, and Foreign Relations
  • Other Notable Police Roles:
    • Executive Officer, PNP Directorate for Human Resource and Doctrine Development
    • Deputy Director for Administration, PNP Intelligence Group
    • City Director, Davao City Police Office
    • Chief, Regional Intelligence and Investigation Division, PNP

Senatorial Record

  • Authored or Co-authored: 22 laws
  • Sponsored/co-sponsored: 20 laws during the 18th Congress

Legislative Accomplishments

  • Republic Act 11549 – PNP, BFP, BJMP and BuCor Height Equality Act: Principal author; ensures inclusive recruitment standards by lowering height restrictions for various law enforcement agencies.
  • Republic Act 11589 – Bureau of Fire Protection Modernization Act: Principal author; aimed at modernizing the Bureau of Fire Protection to improve its capability in fire prevention and suppression.
  • Republic Act 11766 – Fixing the Validity Period of the License to Own, Permit to Carry, and Registration of Firearms: Principal author; simplifies firearm ownership and licensing processes.
  • Republic Act 11926 – Penalizing Wilful and Indiscriminate Discharge of Firearms: Principal author; aims to curb the reckless use of firearms.
  • Republic Act 11917 – Private Security Services Industry Act: Principal author; regulates and modernizes the private security industry.
  • Republic Act 11479 – Anti-Terrorism Act of 2020: Co-sponsored and defended the law, which grants enhanced powers for counterterrorism efforts.
  • Republic Act 11525 – COVID-19 Vaccination Program Act of 2021: Co-sponsored the law aimed at facilitating the country's vaccination efforts.
  • Republic Act 11641 – Department of Migrant Workers Act: Co-authored to establish a new government agency to address the welfare of migrant workers.
  • Republic Act 11691 – Judicial Marshals Act: Co-authored the act to ensure the protection of judicial officers.
  • Republic Act 11712 – COVID-19 Benefits and Allowances for Health Workers Act: Co-authored the act to provide benefits for health workers during the pandemic.

Key Advocacy Areas

  • Drug Control and Rehabilitation: Advocates for both punitive and rehabilitative measures to address drug-related crimes.
  • Law Enforcement Modernization: Focuses on enhancing the capabilities of law enforcement agencies such as the PNP, BFP, and BJMP.
  • National Security and Anti-Terrorism: Strongly supports laws aimed at combating terrorism and insurgency.
  • Support for Uniformed Personnel: Prioritizes the welfare and professional development of uniformed personnel, such as police and firefighters.

Controversies

  • Oplan Tokhang & ICC Investigation: His tenure as PNP Chief, particularly his involvement in the drug war, has been criticized for alleged extrajudicial killings. This led to an ongoing investigation by the International Criminal Court (ICC).
  • Red-Tagging Accusations: Dela Rosa has been accused of red-tagging progressive groups, linking them to insurgent movements without substantial evidence (Palaubsanon, 2024).
  • Populist Rhetoric: Known for his controversial and often emotional remarks, Dela Rosa has faced criticism for his use of populist rhetoric in debates, particularly on national security and the drug war.
