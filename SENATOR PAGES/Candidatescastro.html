Francisca “France” L. Castro

Personal Information

Full Name: Francisca Lustina Castro

Birthdate: May 24, 1966

Age: 58 (as of late 2024/early 2025)

Birthplace: Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines

Current Political Affiliation: Makabayang Koalisyon ng Mamamayan (Makabayan)

Represented Party-list: ACT Teachers Party-list

Political Platforms / Key Advocacy Areas

  • Advocating for increased salaries and benefits for teachers and government employees.
  • Promoting accessible and quality education for all Filipinos.
  • Championing the protection of human rights and civil liberties.
  • Pushing for genuine agrarian reform and national industrialization.
  • Opposing political dynasties and advocating for electoral reforms.

Notable Positions

  • Representative, ACT Teachers Party-list (17th-19th Congress, 2016–Present)
  • Assistant Minority Leader, House of Representatives (18th-19th Congress, 2019–Present)

Legislative Accomplishments (as Representative)

As a representative for the ACT Teachers Party-list, Rep. Castro has been a vocal advocate for teachers' rights, education reforms, and human rights. Notable bills she has authored or co-authored include:

  • House Bill 159: Seeking to strengthen the right of government to expropriate lands for socialized housing purposes.
  • House Bill 252: Aiming to prohibit the establishment and perpetuation of political dynasties.
  • House Bill 253: Proposing the declaration of certain areas as 'no mining zones' to protect communities and the environment.
  • House Bill 254: Pushing for the re-orientation of the Philippine mining industry towards national industrialization and development.
  • House Bill 255: Seeking the creation of the Genuine Small Coconut Farmers’ Fund to support coconut farmers.
