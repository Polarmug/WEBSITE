Senator Christopher "Bong" Go

Personal Information

Full Name: Christopher Lawrence "Bong" Go

Birthdate: June 14, 1974

Age on Election Day: 50

Birthplace: Davao City, Davao del Sur

Residence: Brgy. San Jose, Lupon, Davao Oriental

Sex: Male

Civil Status: Married

Spouse: Emmylou Cruz Go

Profession/Occupation: Senator

Political Party: PDP–Laban

Political Platforms

  • Institutionalizing Malasakit Centers: Advocates for the establishment of one-stop shops for medical assistance in public hospitals nationwide, ensuring easy and free access to healthcare services for Filipinos.
  • Creation of the Department of Disaster Resilience: Proposes the creation of a dedicated government agency for disaster preparedness and response to improve the country’s resilience.
  • Support for Housing and Education: Pushes for initiatives to provide housing and educational assistance to poor families.
  • Infrastructure and Peace Programs: Promotes continued infrastructure development and peace programs, particularly those aligned with the Duterte administration’s legacy.

Notable Positions

  • Special Assistant to the President (2016–2018): Served as the trusted aide to former President Rodrigo Duterte during his tenure as mayor and president.
  • Senator (2019–present): Elected to the Philippine Senate in 2019, ranking third with 20,465,005 votes.

Senatorial Record

  • Authored or co-authored 32 laws and sponsored/co-sponsored 170 laws during the 18th Congress.
  • Known for his focus on healthcare, disaster resilience, and public welfare.

Legislative Accomplishments

  • Republic Act No. 11463 – Malasakit Centers Act of 2019 Role: Principal author and sponsor Contribution: Institutionalized Malasakit Centers, providing accessible medical assistance in public hospitals nationwide.
  • Senate Bill No. 205 – Department of Disaster Resilience Act Role: Principal author Contribution: Proposed the creation of a government agency dedicated to disaster preparedness and response.
  • Republic Act No. 11589 – Bureau of Fire Protection Modernization Act (2021) Role: Co-author Contribution: Supported the modernization of the Bureau of Fire Protection, focusing on capacity building, training, and equipment upgrades.
  • Balik Probinsya, Bagong Pag-Asa Program Role: Proponent and lead advocate Contribution: Pushed for a program aimed at decongesting urban areas and promoting countryside development, a key initiative during the Duterte administration.
  • Republic Act 11861 – Solo Parents Welfare Act Role: Author Contribution: This law provides additional benefits for solo parents, including financial assistance for those earning minimum wage, priority for government housing programs, and discounts on basic necessities.
  • Republic Act 11466 – Salary Standardization Law V Role: Author Contribution: Senator Go was instrumental in the passage of this law, which increases the salaries of civilian government employees, including teachers and nurses.
  • Republic Act 11641 – Department of Migrant Workers Act Role: Principal Author Contribution: This act consolidates all agencies dealing with overseas Filipino workers into a single department, aiming for a more efficient management of migrant workers' welfare.
  • Republic Act 11470 – National Academy of Sports Act Role: Author and Co-sponsor Contribution: This act establishes the National Academy of Sports, aimed at providing world-class training facilities for student-athletes, while also offering them a quality secondary education.

Key Advocacy Areas

  • Healthcare Access: Advocates for the creation of Super Health Centers across the country to bring essential medical services closer to Filipino communities.
  • Disaster Resilience and Response: Calls for the creation of a Department of Disaster Resilience and the construction of mandatory evacuation centers nationwide.
  • Educational and Youth Support: Supports programs such as free college entrance exams and mental health services in public universities.
  • Security and Governance: Promotes initiatives for better governance, including the Magna Carta for Barangay officials and programs to deter drug abuse through sports.

Controversies

  • Malasakit Centers Campaign Allegations: Criticized for allegedly using government-funded Malasakit Centers for premature campaigning, though no formal charges were filed. Since 2018, Bong Go has used the launching of the government’s one-stop shop “Malasakit Center” to bring his name into the forefront—appearing in every photo-op and claiming that it is a project realized through his advocacy. However, Malasakit Centers is an initiative created and funded by multiple government agencies, namely Philippine Charity Sweepstakes Office, Philippine Amusement and Games Corporation, PhilHealth, Department of Health, at Department of Social Work and Development (Rapper, 2019)
  • Frigate Procurement Controversy (2018): Denied involvement in the frigate deal controversy and was cleared by various parties, including former Philippine Navy officials.
  • Philippine Offshore Gaming Operators (POGOs): In 2024, Go expressed opposition to POGOs, despite having previously supported their operations in 2021, raising questions about his changing stance.
