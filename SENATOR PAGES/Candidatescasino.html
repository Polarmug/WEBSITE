Teodoro “Teddy” A. Casiño

Personal Information

Full Name: Teodoro Acevedo Casiño

Birthdate: November 15, 1968

Age: 56 (as of late 2024/early 2025)

Birthplace: Davao City, Philippines

Education: Bachelor of Arts in Sociology, University of the Philippines Los Baños (1993)

Current Political Affiliation: Makabayang Koalisyon ng Mamamayan (Makabayan)

Previous Affiliation: Bayan Muna

Political Platforms

  • Advocates for anti-dynasty legislation, whistleblower protection, freedom of information, and amendments to the Party-list Law.
  • Pushes for the elimination of confidential and intelligence funds and pork barrel allocations in the national budget.
  • Consistently opposes budget cuts in state colleges and universities.
  • Opposes the privatization of public utilities like hospitals and water districts.

Notable Positions

  • Representative, Bayan Muna Party-list (13th-15th Congress, 2004–2013)
  • Committee Roles (during Congressional terms):
    • Chairperson, Committee on Small Business and Entrepreneurship Development
    • Senior Vice Chairperson, Committee on Higher and Technical Education

Legislative Accomplishments (as Representative)

During his tenure in the House of Representatives, Teddy Casiño authored or co-authored significant legislation, including:

  • Public Attorneys Act of 2007 (R.A. 9406): Strengthened the Public Attorney's Office to provide free legal assistance to indigent litigants.
  • Tax Relief Act of 2009 (R.A. 9504): Increased the personal exemption levels for taxpayers, providing economic relief.
  • Rent Control Act of 2009 (R.A. 9653): Provided regulations for rental rates of certain residential units to protect tenants.
  • Anti-Torture Act of 2009 (R.A. 9745): Penalizes acts of torture and other cruel, inhuman, and degrading treatment or punishment.

Controversies

  • Has faced legal challenges and accusations of affiliation with communist groups, which he has consistently denied (often referred to as "red-tagging").
  • In 2017, he and other activists were charged with breach of peace, assault upon an agent of authority, and disobedience following a protest against the ASEAN Summit held in Manila.
  • In 2023, Casiño filed a civil lawsuit against hosts of Sonshine Media Network International (SMNI) for alleged red-tagging and defamation.
