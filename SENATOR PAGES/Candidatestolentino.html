Senator Francis "Tol" Tolentino

Personal Information

Full Name: Francis Ng Tolentino

Birthdate: January 2, 1960

Age on Election Day: 65

Birthplace: Albay, Philippines

Residence: Tagaytay City, Cavite

Sex: Male

Civil Status: Married

Profession/Occupation: Lawyer / Politician / Law Professor

Political Party: Partido Federal ng Pilipinas (PFP)

Platforms

  • Advocates for access to clean water, universal Wi-Fi, and affordable electricity
  • Introduced the LITAW (Liwanag, Internet, Tubig Assistance Welfare) program to assist families displaced by calamities
  • Supports the Philippine Maritime Zones Act to assert the country's territorial claims in the West Philippine Sea
  • Emphasizes the importance of choosing candidates with integrity, a solid track record, and clear governance platforms

Notable Positions

  • Senator of the Philippines (2019–present)
  • Senate Majority Leader (2023–present)
  • Chairman, Senate Committees on Justice and Human Rights, and Accountability of Public Officers and Investigations (Blue Ribbon Committee)
  • Chairman, Metro Manila Development Authority (2010–2015)
  • Mayor of Tagaytay City (1995–2004)

Notable Laws and Projects

  • Authored Senate Bill No. 2492, the Philippine Maritime Zones Act, to define the country's maritime entitlements
  • Launched the LITAW program to provide essential services to disaster-affected families

Controversies

  • In 2015, faced criticism over the performance of the dance group "Playgirls" at a public event, leading to his resignation as MMDA Chairman
  • In 2024, resigned from the Partido ng Demokratiko Pilipino (PDP) due to differing views on the West Philippine Sea policy
