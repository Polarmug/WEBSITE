Francis "Kiko" Pangilinan

Personal Information

Full Name: Francis Pancratius Nepomuceno Pangilinan

Birthdate: August 24, 1963

Age on Election Day: 61

Birthplace: Manila, Philippines

Residence: Brgy. Inchican, Silang, Cavite

Sex: Male

Civil Status: Married

Spouse: Sharon Cuneta (actress and singer)

Children: 3, including KC Concepcion (stepdaughter)

Profession/Occupation: Lawyer / Politician / Farm Owner

Political Party: Liberal Party of the Philippines

Platforms

  • Advocates for lowering food prices and improving food security
  • Supports increasing the minimum wage and addressing primary concerns of the masses
  • Promotes agricultural development and modernization
  • Emphasizes the importance of transparency and accountability in government operations

Notable Positions

  • Senator of the Philippines (2001–2013; 2016–2022)
  • Senate Majority Leader (2004–2008)
  • Chairman, Senate Committees on Agriculture and Food; Agrarian Reform; Constitutional Amendments and Revision of Codes
  • Presidential Assistant for Food Security and Agricultural Modernization (2014–2015)
  • Member, Quezon City Council (1988–1992)
  • President, Liberal Party of the Philippines (2022–present)

Notable Laws and Projects

  • Doubled the budget and staff complement of the Fertilizer and Pesticide Authority to meet increasing regulatory demands
  • Pushed for a 52% increase in the agriculture budget in 2012 after initiating the Agriculture and Fisheries 2025 summit
  • Bridged market, training, and credit gaps in the food supply chain for farmer-beneficiaries through the pilot Sagip Saka program
  • Supported the construction of 200 school buildings/classrooms across 45 provinces in collaboration with the Filipino Chinese Chamber of Commerce
  • Authored the Judiciary Modernization and Compensation Act (RA 9227), doubling the pay and benefits of judiciary members
  • Proposed the creation of the Judicial Executive, Legislative, Advisory and Consultative Council (JELACC) to address judiciary budgetary and infrastructure challenges

Controversies

  • In 2022, ran for vice president alongside Leni Robredo but lost to Sara Duterte
  • Faced criticism from political opponents for his stance on various issues, including his opposition to certain policies of the Marcos and Duterte administrations
