Senator Imee Marcos

Personal Information

Full Name: Maria Imelda Josefa Remedios "Imee" Romualdez Marcos

Birthdate: November 12, 1955

Age on Election Day: 69

Birthplace: Manila, Philippines

Residence: Batac, Ilocos Norte

Sex: Female

Civil Status: Divorced

Profession/Occupation: Politician / Former Actress / Film Producer

Political Party: Independent (formerly Nacionalista Party)

Platforms

  • Advocates for a uniform minimum wage across all regions to promote equitable economic growth
  • Supports increased funding for agriculture, aiming to strengthen the entire supply chain from production to distribution
  • Promotes national sovereignty, expressing concerns over foreign military presence and its implications on Philippine security
  • Endorses digital innovation and creative industries to boost employment and economic development

Notable Positions

  • Senator of the Philippines (2019–present)
  • Governor of Ilocos Norte (2010–2019)
  • Representative, Ilocos Norte's 2nd District (1998–2007)

Senatorial Record

  • Authored/Co-Authored: Over 8 laws during her tenureship as a senator. However, she has filed over 339 senate house bills.

Notable Laws and Projects

  • Introduced a Seven-Point Plan focusing on economic recovery, digital transformation, and cultural preservation
  • Championed legislation to support local farmers and fisherfolk, aiming to enhance food security and rural development

Controversies

  • Faced criticism for her TikTok video alleging Chinese missile threats, which was deemed unverified by national security officials
  • Experienced a public rift with her brother, President Ferdinand Marcos Jr., leading to her withdrawal from the administration's senatorial slate
  • Released a campaign ad titled "Itim" (Black), which drew backlash from activist groups who viewed it as a negative portrayal of the country's future under continued Marcos-Duterte leadership
  • Investigations into her handling of public funds—most prominently, the controversial use of tobacco excise tax in pet projects during her governorship—continue to contribute to her corrupt image.
