Erwin Teshiba Tulfo

Personal Information

Full Name: Erwin Teshiba Tulfo

Birthdate: August 10, 1963

Age on Election Day: 61

Birthplace: Tacloban City, Leyte

Residence: Brgy. Paligsahan, Quezon City, Metro Manila

Sex: Male

Civil Status: Married

Spouse: Rizalina Aquino

Profession/Occupation: Journalist / Congressman

Political Party: Lakas-CMD / ACT-CIS

Political Platforms

  • Mandatory Annual Physical Exams for Women: Advocates for a law mandating free annual physical exams for women aged 30 and above (House Bill No. 8989).
  • Digital Transformation of Government Services: Supports the modernization of government services to make them more accessible and efficient through digital platforms.
  • 24/7 Infrastructure Construction: Proposes round-the-clock construction schedules to accelerate the completion of key infrastructure projects.
  • Empowering Local Governments: Focuses on strengthening basic services by empowering local government units (LGUs) to better serve their constituents.

Notable Positions

  • Current Position:
    • Party-list Representative (ACT-CIS, 2023–Present)
    • Deputy House Majority Leader (2023–Present)
  • Previous Position:
    • Secretary of the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD), June–December 2022
  • Other Occupation: TV and radio broadcaster, host of "Tutok Tulfo"

Senatorial Record (IF ANY)

None (Tulfo, E., is a first-time senatorial candidate in 2025)

Legislative Accomplishments

As Secretary of the DSWD (2022):

  • Led relief efforts for disaster-stricken communities, including fire victims in Quezon City.
  • Collaborated with the Anti-Red Tape Authority (ARTA) to expedite processes for disaster-related foreign donations.
  • Played a key role in providing financial aid to marginalized sectors, such as indigenous peoples and solo parents.
  • “Digital Assistance Card” under DSWD (2022): Key implementer, proposing a centralized digital ID system for efficient distribution of aid.

As a Lawmaker:

  • House Bill No. 8989 (Mandatory Annual Physical Exam for Women Act): Principal author of this bill, advocating for free annual physical exams for women aged 30 and above.
  • House Resolution No. 1261 (Investigation into LGU Budget Utilization): Principal author, calling for an audit of LGUs’ spending on assistance programs.
  • Support for SOGIE Equality Bill: Expressed support for the SOGIE Equality Bill during his 2024 interpellation in Congress.
  • Co-Author for the House Bill 10212: Erwin Tulfo, along with Rep. Ralph and Jocelyn Tulfo filed HB 10212, a house bill that would strengthen the Commission on Human Rights—helping it embody the will of the Paris Principles.

Key Advocacy Areas

  • Women’s Health and Rights: Advocates for accessible healthcare for women, especially through mandatory physical exams.
  • Digital Government Services: Pushes for modernized and accessible government systems through digital transformation.
  • Infrastructure Development: Focuses on enhancing infrastructure, with a particular emphasis on accelerating projects through round-the-clock construction.
  • Local Governance Empowerment: Aims to strengthen the role and capacity of LGUs in delivering public services.

Controversies

  • Disqualification Case Filed (February 2025):

    A petition filed by lawyer Berteni Causing seeks to disqualify Tulfo from the 2025 Senate race on grounds including a 2008 libel conviction and questions regarding his citizenship and academic qualifications.

    COMELEC, on March 13, 2025, junked the second petition for disqualification that Bertini filed, citing failure to submit sufficient or complete evidence to all relevant authorities (Manabat, 2025).

  • Citizenship and Identity Fraud Allegations:

    Accused of failing to renounce his U.S. citizenship and obtaining an American passport under the name “Erich Sylvester Tulfo.” Tulfo has not fully addressed these allegations (Oliquino, 2025).

    His DSWD appointment was bypassed in 2022 due to these issues.

  • Admission of TNT Status in the U.S.:

    Tulfo admitted to being an undocumented immigrant (TNT) in the U.S. for about ten years before becoming a U.S. citizen in the late 1980s (Oliquino, 2025).

  • Political Dynasty Concerns:

    Critics argue that his senatorial run, alongside his brother Senator Raffy Tulfo, violates the 1987 Constitution’s anti-political dynasty provision.

    Despite this, Tulfo supports anti-political dynasty legislation, stating that his family agrees with reforms to give others a chance (Lalu, 2025).
